What Happened?

As one of the longest ships in the world, Ever Given, while sailing on the Suez Canal in March 2021, the long-sending liner turned to the side and ran aground due to strong winds, causing the Suez Canal navigation to be blocked.

Ever Given Given stranded and stuck on Suez Canal, paralyzed Eurasian channel

The shipping company Evergreen Shipping received a notice from the owner of the chartered vessel Ever Given that the container vessel entered the Suez Canal from the north of the Red Sea at around 8 am Egypt time on March 23 (14:00 on the same day, Taipei time), and was at the southern end of the estuary. At 6 nautical miles, it was suspected of being hit by a strong wind instantly, causing the hull to deviate from the channel and accidentally bottomed and ran aground.

Evergreen has urged the shipowner to report the cause of the accident, and worked out a plan with related units such as the Canal Administration to assist the ship in getting out of trouble as soon as possible. Ever Given is a 20,000 TEU-class container ship, which is chartered by Evergreen Shipping in the form of Time Charter. The crew is dispatched by the shipowner company. The ship is currently deployed on the Far East-Europe route.
